### Subject: The Feasibility of Purchasing Food on Campus 
The expensive pricing of all food on campus, both in shops and restaurants, is causing 
increasing dissatisfaction among students. This report aims to outline the reasons behind this 
frustration, evaluating current pricing strategies and identifying possible solutions to decrease 
food costs on students. 

### Recommendation 
Given the current rising expense of food shopping on Irish students, it is recommended that on
campus vendors should decrease the price of their goods substantially. Rebellion from students 
is becoming increasingly prevalent as prices surge. 

### Data 
All goods and services for sale in Ireland are currently on the rise, with food prices being a 
growing topic of concern among Irish citizens. As of this month, October 2025, grocery price 
inflation has reached its highest level since December 2023 at 6.5% (Wordpanel by 
Numerator). In conjunction with heightened grocery expenses, the Central Statistics Office has 
found that September 2025 had an 18-month high overall inflation rise of 2.7%. Therefore, 
students who are already paying high tuition fees, rent and utilities must now also bear paying 
more for the same amount of food in order to survive.  

### University of Limerick Food Issues 
Prices on UL campus are shockingly high, with most cafes and restaurants, such as Stables and 
Eden, having meals priced between €8- €12. This is a substantial cost when considering the 
fact that minimum wage is just €13.50 per hour in 2025. As well as this, Spar is the only shop 
available to students on campus and hence monopolises the grocery market. They charge 
extreme prices without any nearby competition to undercut them. This is a large root cause of 
student frustration with current pricing. 

### Solutions 
The continuous rise of food prices on Irish university goers must be reversed. All campuses 
should look to introduce new grocery shops to their respective campuses, leading to 
competitive pricing strategies and thus cutting prices for students. Temporary caps on essential 
food items should also be considered, preventing further growth of prices. 
Irish students should continue their effort to lobby universities and call for lower grocery prices. 
Immediate intervention is required by these institutions before undergraduates are forced into 
extreme circumstances.
